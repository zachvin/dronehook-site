What we do

We're Dronehook, a student-led and NASA-funded research team working to combine the range of a fixed-wing UAV with the package pickup capabilities of a quadcopter. A fully-autonomous vision- and gps-based system, Dronehook will be able to collect a package from a portable ground station without the need for landing. As a result, Dronehook is an efficient package retrieval device that does not require significant infrastructure or a long runway.

How it works

Takeoff

Dronehook first takes off from a runway at a designated takeoff location.

Navigation

Dronehook then autonomously flies to a specified GPS location for package pickup.

Pickup

Dronehook switches to computer vision to guide itself toward the Home Station. Dronehook then flies over the Home Station, collecting the package in stride.

Delivery

Finally, Dronehook delivers the package to another specified GPS location.

Repeat

The advantage of Dronehook is that its package collection and dropoff are totally autonomous, allowing for a string of consecutive pickups with zero human intervention.
